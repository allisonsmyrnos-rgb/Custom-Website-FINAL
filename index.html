    A Background of Ballet Companies

    A ballet company is a professional performing arts organization made up of trained dancers, artistic staff, and a production crew who all work together to create and stage full length productions of classical ballets and contemporary works. The dancers in these companies work full time for the company and are ranked based off of their training and time in the company.

    New York City Ballet

    The New York City Ballet (NYCB) is one of the foremost dance companies in the United States. Founded in 1948 by choreographer George Balanchine and Lincoln Kirstein, the company is renowned for its strong classical technique and innovative contemporary works. NYCB performs a wide repertoire, including Balanchine's masterpieces and new works by contemporary choreographers.

    American Ballet Theatre

    American Ballet Theatre (ABT) is another leading ballet company in the United States, known for its diverse repertoire that spans classical, neoclassical, and contemporary works. Founded in 1939, ABT has a long history of presenting world-class performances and nurturing talented dancers who go on to have successful careers in ballet.

    San Francisco Ballet

    San Francisco Ballet, founded in 1933, is the oldest professional ballet company in the United States. It is renowned for its innovative programming and commitment to both classical and contemporary works. The company has a strong reputation for technical excellence and artistic creativity, attracting top dancers and choreographers from around the world.

    Boston Ballet

    Boston Ballet, founded in 1963, is one of the leading ballet companies in the United States. It is known for its diverse repertoire, which includes classical, contemporary, and full-length story ballets. The company has a strong commitment to education and community engagement, providing training and performance opportunities for dancers of all ages.

    Houston Ballet

    Houston Ballet, founded in 1955, is a prominent ballet company in the United States. It is recognized for its innovative programming, which includes classical, contemporary, and full-length story ballets. The company has a strong focus on education and community outreach, offering training programs and performance opportunities for dancers of all levels.
